Introduce the problem and define key terms

Weddings are generally regarded as expensive; according to a survey published last year by The Knot, the national average cost of a wedding was $30,000. (Stations, 2023)Additionally, people think sustainable products as lower quality, less aesthetically pleasing, and more expensive. (White, 2023) Therefore, we aim to determine if we can provide cost-effective weddings to customers.

  1. Wedding Vendors: These include a wide range of businesses crucial to the wedding ceremony or celebration, such as venues, catering, florists, photography, and more. Our Wedding database comprises ten departments, all essential for the smooth execution of a wedding event.
  2. Sustainable: In the most broad way, sustainability refers to the ability to continuously maintain or support a process over time. In business and policy contexts, sustainability seeks to prevent the depletion of natural or physical resources in order to ensure their long-term availability. In this case, each vendor is labeled with vendor_sustainable, with 'yes' representing 1 and 'no' representing 0. (Mollenkamp, 2023)
  3. Cost-effective: It refers to good value for the money spent. (Cost-effective, 2023)

Answer the business question

We compared the cost-effectiveness of service providers with and without sustainable practices using the Student's t-test on the wedding database. Because the p-value (9.952837709511204e-05) was less than a significance level of 0.05. Hence, the null hypothesis was rejected, indicating a statistically significant difference in cost-effectiveness. The findings show that in our sample, vendors who practice sustainable practices have a significant cost-effectiveness advantage. This implies that investing in sustainability may benefit vendors' cost-effectiveness.

In summary, the data indicate that a significant portion of vendors (around 55%) are recognized as sustainable, with significant variation. The average vendor rating is 37.85 (out of a possible 50) with a standard deviation of 19.74. The 1586 average price, along with a higher standard deviation. Also, the mean of cost-effectiveness is 2.29. This comprehensive overview reveals the central tendencies, distribution, and variability of sustainability, ratings, price, and cost-effectiveness, laying the foundations for further research and analysis.

from scipy.stats import ttest_ind

# Divide wedding database into two categories: vendors who use sustainable practices and those who don't.
sustainable_group = wedding[wedding['vendor_sustainable'] == 1]['price_ce']
non_sustainable_group = wedding[wedding['vendor_sustainable'] == 0]['price_ce']

# ttest
t_statistic, p_value = ttest_ind(sustainable_group, non_sustainable_group)

# result 
print(f"T-statistic: {t_statistic}")
print(f"P-value: {p_value}")

# Significance level of 0.05
alpha = 0.05
if p_value < alpha:
    print("""Cost-effectiveness differences among vendors with sustainable practices were 
found to be statistically significant.""")
else:
    print("""Cost-effectiveness differences observed among vendors 
with sustainable practices are not statistically significant.""")
